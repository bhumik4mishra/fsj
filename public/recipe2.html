Stuffed Brinjal

Bharale Vange is a delicious Maharashtrian dish made using brinjals (eggplants) stuffed with a flavorful peanut and spice mixture. The stuffed brinjals are cooked slowly until they become tender and absorb all the flavors of the masala.

  1. Prepare the stuffing: Mix roasted peanut powder, Sunday masala, red chili powder, salt, turmeric powder, and finely chopped coriander leaves. Add a little water gradually to form a thick paste. This masala will be used to stuff the brinjals.
  2. Wash the brinjals: Wash and dry the brinjals thoroughly.
  3. Prepare the brinjals: Cut off the stem of each brinjal and make two slits in a cross shape. Do not cut them completely.
  4. Stuff the brinjals: Sprinkle a little salt inside each brinjal and carefully stuff them with the prepared masala.
  5. Seal the brinjals: Press the brinjals gently from all sides so that the stuffing stays inside.
  6. Heat oil: Heat oil in a deep pan and add cumin seeds. Allow them to splutter.

  9. Add leftover stuffing: Add any leftover stuffing mixture to the pan. Pour a little water and mix well to form a thick gravy.
  10. Add brinjals: Carefully place the stuffed brinjals into the gravy.
  11. Steam cook: Cover the pan and cook the brinjals on medium heat for 7‚Äì8 minutes.
  12. Flip and cook: Gently flip the brinjals and steam them again for another 7‚Äì8 minutes.
  13. Repeat: Continue flipping and steaming until the brinjals are cooked thoroughly and tender.
  14. Garnish and serve: Turn off the heat and garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves. Serve hot with chapati or bhakri.
