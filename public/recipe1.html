Cumcumber Paratha

To make cucumber paratha, you need wheat flour, grated cucumber, chopped green chilies, and various spices like salt, turmeric, cumin, and red chili powder. You'll mix the ingredients to form a dough, then shape and cook the parathas on a hot griddle (tawa) using oil or ghee until golden brown on both sides.

  • 2 cups wheat flour (atta)
  • 1 cup grated cucumber
  • 2 finely chopped green chilies
  • 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves
  • 1 tsp cumin powder (jeera powder)
  • 1 tsp chaat masala (optional)
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Water, as needed
  • Oil or ghee, for cooking
  • Optional: 1 tbsp curd, 1 tsp ginger paste, 1/2 tsp turmeric powder, 1 tsp carom seeds (ajwain), 1 tbsp semolina (sooji)

  1. Prepare the Dough: In a large bowl, combine the wheat flour, grated cucumber (after squeezing out excess water), green chilies, coriander leaves, and all the dry spices.
  2. Knead: Add water gradually, a little at a time, and mix to form a soft, medium-consistency dough.
  3. Rest the Dough: Cover the dough and let it rest for about 15-20 minutes.
  4. Roll the Parathas: Divide the dough into small balls. Dust each ball with flour and roll it into a round paratha of your desired thickness.
  5. Cook: Heat a griddle (tawa) over medium heat. Place the rolled paratha on the hot tawa.
  6. Roast: Cook on one side until it starts to brown, then flip it.
  7. Finish: Apply ghee or oil on both sides and cook, pressing gently with a spatula, until golden brown and fully cooked.
  8. Serve: Serve the hot cucumber paratha with yogurt (raita), pickles, or chutney.
